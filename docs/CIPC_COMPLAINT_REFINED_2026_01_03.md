# CIPC Companies Act Complaint (Refined)

**Date of Submission:** 2026-01-05  
**Case Reference:** 2025-137857

## 1. Introduction

This complaint details serious violations of the Companies Act 71 of 2008 by directors and related persons of RegimA Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd (2009/016244/07), RegimA Skin Treatments (Pty) Ltd (2003/017754/07), and other associated entities. The evidence points to a coordinated scheme of fraudulent activities, director misconduct, and financial misrepresentation.

**Total Criminal Threshold Events:** 46

## 2. Parties

- **Complainant:** Daniel James Faucitt (on behalf of RegimA Zone Ltd)
- **Primary Subjects of Complaint:**
  - **Peter Andrew Faucitt:** Director, Trustee
  - **Rynette Farrar:** Financial Controller, de facto director
  - **Danie Bantjies:** Accountant, Trustee

## 3. Summary of Violations

| Section of Companies Act | Violation | Perpetrator(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Section 22** | Reckless and Fraudulent Trading | Peter Faucitt, Rynette Farrar |
| **Section 76** | Breach of Director's Fiduciary Duties | Peter Faucitt |
| **Section 77** | Liability of Directors for Losses | Peter Faucitt |
| **Section 214** | False Statements and Misrepresentation | Peter Faucitt, Rynette Farrar, Danie Bantjies |

## 4. Detailed Allegations & Evidence

### 4.1. Reckless and Fraudulent Trading (Section 22)

**Allegation:** The directors and controllers knowingly carried on the business recklessly, with gross negligence, and with intent to defraud creditors and stakeholders. This includes the systematic diversion of revenue streams from the legitimate platform owner (RegimA Zone Ltd) to entities controlled by the perpetrators.

**Evidence:**
- **JF07 - Payment Redirection:** 186 items documenting the unauthorized redirection of payments.
- **JF08 - Comprehensive Fraud Timeline:** Details the sequence of fraudulent transactions.
- **SF3 - Stock Fraud:** R5.4M stock adjustment to conceal financial realities.

### 4.2. Breach of Director's Fiduciary Duties (Section 76)

**Allegation:** Peter Faucitt failed to act in good faith and in the best interests of the company. He used his position to enrich himself, allowed the misappropriation of company assets, and failed to prevent the fraudulent activities of Rynette Farrar.

**Evidence:**
- **Faucitt Family Trust Documents:** Showing his duties as a trustee.
- **Financial Records (JF03):** Demonstrating unauthorized transfers and personal enrichment.
- **SF9 - Ian Levitt Attorney Memo:** Documents his failure to act on legal advice and address fraud allegations.

### 4.3. False Statements and Misrepresentation (Section 214)

**Allegation:** The perpetrators knowingly provided false and misleading financial information to stakeholders, auditors, and legal representatives. Danie Bantjies, as the accountant, was central to this misrepresentation, preparing and distributing false trial balances and concealing the true financial state of the companies.

**Evidence:**
- **Trial Balance Documents:** Distributed by Danie Bantjies, showing manipulated figures.
- **SF1 - Bantjies Debt Documentation:** Establishes his R18.685M conflict of interest, providing a motive for concealing the fraud.
- **SF4 - SARS Audit Email:** Evidence of coordination to mislead tax authorities.

## 5. Conclusion

The evidence demonstrates a clear and sustained pattern of criminal conduct in violation of the Companies Act. We request a full investigation by the CIPC into the conduct of the directors and de facto controllers of the implicated companies.

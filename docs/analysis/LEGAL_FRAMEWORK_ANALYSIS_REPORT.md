## 1. Legal Theories Framework

The narrative supports four major juridical categories, each with distinct legal foundations and elements:

### 1.1. Theory 001: Abuse of Ex Parte Procedure

**Legal Foundation:**
- *Schlesinger v Schlesinger* 1979 (4) SA 342 (W)
- *NDPP v Basson*
- *Powell v Van der Merwe*
- Uniform Rule 6(4)

**Elements:**
1. Duty of utmost good faith (*uberrima fides*)
2. Material non-disclosure
3. Suppression of adverse facts
4. Facts discoverable with minimal diligence

**Narrative Omissions Identified:**
- Actual control structure (Bantjies exercising financial control)
- Jacqui reacting to debt owed to Kayla's estate
- Retaliation chronology (card cancellations, system sabotage)
- UK → SA payment flows (not SA → UK as claimed)
- Revenues outweighed the "unexplainable IT expenses"
- Daniel's role in UK compliance, logistics, and operations

**Events Supporting This Theory:** 3 events mapped

**Legal Articulation:**

> "The founding affidavit suppressed facts essential to the court's evaluation of prejudice, urgency, balance of convenience, and locus standi. The omissions were material because disclosure would have undermined the asserted basis for interim relief."

## 2. Interim Interdict Test Analysis

The narrative provides counter-arguments to each element of the interim interdict test:

### 2.1. Prima Facie Right

**Applicant's Claim:** Peter Faucitt had operational authority and right to control

**Counter-Argument Based on Evidence:**
- Peter had no operational role
- Financial systems controlled by Bantjies and Rynette
- Daniel and Jacqui fulfilled fiduciary duties
- Shopify platform owned and paid by Daniel's UK company (RegimA Zone Ltd)

**Legal Articulation:**

> "The applicant lacked the prima facie right he asserted: the factual substratum of control, authority, and operational responsibility was misrepresented."

**Supporting Events:** Multiple control-related events showing Rynette's financial control, Bantjies' trustee role

---

### 2.2. Reasonable Apprehension of Irreparable Harm

**Applicant's Claim:** Respondents' actions would cause irreparable harm to business

**Counter-Argument Based on Evidence:**
- Harm flowed from applicant's side (card cancellations, system sabotage)
- Applicant manufactured the harm to ground urgency
- 6 months of systematic sabotage documented (March - September 2025)

**Legal Articulation:**

> "The applicant manufactured the harm relied on to ground urgency."

**Supporting Events:** 8 sabotage and retaliation events

---

### 2.3. Balance of Convenience

**Applicant's Claim:** Balance favors granting interdict

**Counter-Argument Based on Evidence:**
- Jacqui is the only Responsible Person for EU compliance
- Daniel and Jacqui operated R1M+ monthly revenue streams
- Interdict caused far greater harm than it prevented
- Business operations destroyed by interdict

**Legal Articulation:**

> "The balance of convenience overwhelmingly disfavors the interdict."

**Supporting Events:** Revenue generation events, operational necessity events

---

### 2.4. No Adequate Alternative Remedy

**Applicant's Claim:** No alternative remedy available

**Counter-Argument Based on Evidence:**
- Internal remedies were available
- Mediation was underway
- Company structures allowed for resolution

**Legal Articulation:**

> "The interdict was not the least restrictive means and thus legally disproportionate."

**Supporting Events:** Mediation and internal resolution attempts

# POPIA Criminal Complaint

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-14

This document outlines the criminal complaint to be filed with the Information Regulator regarding the violations of the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) by the perpetrators in case 2025-137857.

---

## Key Violations

### Unauthorized Access to Personal Information

The perpetrators gained unauthorized access to personal information, including the email accounts and personal data of the victims and their clients. This information was then used to perpetrate the fraud.

**Evidence:**
- **[SF2: Sage Screenshots - Rynette Control](../../evidence/critical.md#sf2-sage-screenshots---rynette-control):** Proves unauthorized access to email accounts.
- **[JF01: Shopify Plus Email](../../evidence/critical.md#jf01-shopify-plus-email-26-july-2017---the-forensic-time-capsule):** Contains personal information that was accessed and misused.

### Failure to Protect Personal Information

The perpetrators, in their capacity as directors and officers of the companies, failed to implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the personal information of their clients and employees, as required by POPIA.

**Evidence:**
- The very fact that the data breach and fraud occurred is evidence of the failure to protect personal information.
- The lack of security measures and the ease with which the perpetrators were able to access and misuse the data will be detailed in the full complaint.
